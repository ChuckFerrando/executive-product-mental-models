# Post-AI Judgment Layer

## Definition

The layer of human decision-making that sits on top of AI-generated outputs, where judgment, context, ethics, and product thinking determine final outcomes.

## Core Principle

> AI produces outputs. Humans determine what those outputs mean and what to do next.

## System Structure

1. AI Output Layer
   - Generated responses
   - Predictions
   - Recommendations

2. Judgment Layer (Human)
   - Interpretation
   - Tradeoff evaluation
   - Risk assessment
   - Ethical consideration
   - Customer impact

3. Decision Layer
   - Final action
   - Product direction
   - Implementation choices

## Key Insight

AI compresses execution, but amplifies the importance of judgment.

## Failure Mode

Most practitioners:
- stop at the output layer
- optimize for speed and volume
- fail to demonstrate decision quality

## Differentiation

High-performing leaders:
- interrogate outputs
- apply structured judgment
- connect outputs to user outcomes
- own final decisions

## Application

Use this model to:
- evaluate candidates
- structure interviews
- design AI-enabled workflows
- train teams on AI usage

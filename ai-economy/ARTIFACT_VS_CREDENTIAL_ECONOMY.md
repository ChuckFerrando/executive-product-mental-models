# Artifact vs Credential Economy

## Definition

The transition from a **credential-based economy** to an **artifact-based economy**, where demonstrable outputs and real-world work replace degrees and certifications as the primary signal of capability.

## Core Principle

> In AI-driven environments, what you have built matters more than what you have been certified in.

## Two Systems

### Credential Economy
- Degrees
- Certifications
- Course completion
- Resume signals
- Low verification of real capability

### Artifact Economy
- Built systems
- Prototypes
- Experiments
- Evaluation frameworks
- Real outputs and results
- High verification of capability

## Key Shift

| Dimension | Credential Economy | Artifact Economy |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| Signal Type | Indirect | Direct |
| Trust Level | Low | High |
| Verification | Weak | Strong |
| Hiring Focus | Background | Output |

## Why This Shift Is Happening

- AI tools compress execution → output becomes easier to produce
- Employers need proof of judgment, not exposure
- Traditional education does not map to real-world AI workflows
- The cost of verifying capability through artifacts is lower than ever

## Implications

- Candidates must build, not just learn
- Portfolios replace resumes as primary signal
- Evaluation and judgment become core skills
- Continuous creation becomes a competitive advantage

## Application

Use this model to:

- Design hiring systems
- Evaluate candidates
- Structure personal learning
- Build portfolio strategy
